Touch typing is the ability to type without looking at the keyboard using home-row finger placement and muscle memory. It’s unglamorous but yields faster capture, fewer interruptions, and less cognitive load.

Why it matters
- Speed and fluency: touch typing increases words-per-minute and preserves writing momentum.
- Lower cognitive load: your visual attention stays on content, not key-hunting.
- Accuracy and ergonomics: consistent technique reduces errors and awkward motions.

How to learn
Use short daily practice with modern tutors (Keybr, TypingClub, Monkeytype) or gamified drills. Try a simple progression: home row (1–2 weeks), add other rows (3–4 weeks), then practice real text and timed tests. Ten minutes daily is enough to see steady gains.

Rules that help
- Slow is fast: build accuracy before chasing speed.
- Short, regular sessions beat occasional marathons.
- Keep eyes on the screen; use a keyboard cover if needed.
- Maintain neutral wrists and good posture.

Where it shines
For writing, coding, and live note-taking, touch typing remains portable, private, and immediate. Start with ten minutes daily and track progress weekly.

Happy typing.

Ideas often arrive faster than our fingers. The point of "Thought to Screen" is simple: make capture fast and forgiving so thoughts survive long enough to be shaped.

Externalizing — writing, sketching, or a quick voice memo — offloads working memory and clarifies thinking. Visible notes let you test connections, spot gaps, and iterate more effectively.

Typing can be the bottleneck: many people type 30–50 WPM while speech and thought flow faster. Dictation or short audio notes are excellent for trapping ideas; editing comes later. Prioritize capture over polish.

Practical tips:
- Capture fast: spend 60–90 seconds to get a skeleton into a note or voice memo.
- Keep tools minimal: one-tap capture, simple editors, and keyboard shortcuts.
- Mix modes: dictate rough drafts, then refine by typing.
- Use templates/snippets for recurring idea types.
- Practice short typing sessions to lower mechanical friction.

Design your capture flow to remove friction (no logins, fast save). Emerging tech like better dictation and early BCIs will shift interfaces, but habit wins today: a trivial capture routine turns fleeting sparks into drafts.

Happy typing — and happy thinking.
